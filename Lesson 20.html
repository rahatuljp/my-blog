2️⃣ Proper use of the polite style or the plain style

Polite Style: Versatile and safe for most situations, including speaking with strangers, superiors, or acquaintances. It is appropriate even when speaking to those younger or lower in rank if the relationship is not close.
Plain Style: Used for close friends, family, and colleagues. Using this style inappropriately can seem impolite, so when in doubt, use the polite style.
Written Context: The plain style is standard for formal writing, including books, newspapers, and diaries, while polite style is typically used in letters.

3️⃣ Conversation in the plain style

1: Questions in the Plain Style

Questions in the plain style generally omit the particle か, which denotes a question, and end with a rising intonation, such as のむ(⤴).

コーヒー を 飲のむ？

Koohii wo nomu?

কফি খাবে?

……うん、飲のむ。

......Un, nomu.

......হ্যাঁ, খাব।

2: Noun and な-Adjective Questions

In noun and な-adjective questions, だ, which is the plain form of です, is omitted. In an answer in the affirmative, ending the sentence with だ could sound too rough. You can either omit だ or add some sentence final particle to soften the tone of the sentence. Women seldom use だ.

今晩こんばん 暇ひま？

Konban hima?

আজ রাতে কি ফ্রি আছ?

……うん、暇ひま／暇ひまだ／暇ひまだよ。

......Un, hima / hima da / hima da yo.

......হ্যাঁ, ফ্রি। (পুরুষদের ব্যবহার্য)

……うん、暇ひま／暇ひまよ。

......Un, hima / hima yo.

......হ্যাঁ, ফ্রি। (নারীদের ব্যবহার্য)

……ううん、暇ひまじゃ ない。

......Uun, hima ja nai.

......না, ফ্রি নই।

3: Omission of Particles

In the plain style, certain particles are often omitted if the meaning of the sentence is evident from the context.
で, に, から, まで, と, etc., however, are not omitted because the meaning of the sentence may not be clear without them.

ごはん［を］食たべる？

Gohan [wo] taberu?

ভাত/খাবার খাবে?

あした 京都きょうと［へ］行いかない？

Ashita Kyoto [he] ikanai?

আগামীকাল আমার সাথে কিয়োটো যাবে না?

この りんご［は］おいしいね。

Kono ringo [wa] oishii ne.

এই আপেলটি সুস্বাদু, তাই না?

そこに はさみ［が］ある？

Soko ni hasami [ga] aru?

ওখানে কি কাঁচি আছে?

4: Omission of い in て-form

In the plain style, い of V て-form いる is also often dropped.

辞書じしょ、持もって［い］る？

Jisho, motte [i]ru?

তোমার কাছে কি অভিধান আছে?

……うん、持もって［い］る。

......Un, motte [i]ru.

......হ্যাঁ, আছে।

……ううん、持もって［い］ない。

......Uun, motte [i]nai.

......না, নেই।
